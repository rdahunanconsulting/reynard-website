Privacy Notice

Reynard Dahunan – Privacy & Compliance Consulting values your right to privacy. This Privacy Notice explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal data in compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) and applicable issuances of the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

1) Personal Data We Collect

Information you voluntarily provide when you contact us or request our services, which may include:

  • Identity & contact details (e.g., name, email, phone, organization, role)
  • Message content and any files you send related to your inquiry or engagement
  • Basic technical data (e.g., IP address, timestamp) captured by standard server logs

2) Purpose & Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data only for legitimate purposes, including:

  • Responding to inquiries, scheduling calls, and providing proposals or statements of work
  • Delivering consulting services and managing client relationships
  • Fulfilling legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations

Legal basis: your consent, performance of a contract (or steps prior to entering one), and compliance with legal obligations.

3) Data Sharing & International Transfers

We do not sell personal data. We may share data with trusted service providers (e.g., email or cloud hosting) under confidentiality and data protection commitments, or when required by law or competent authority. If data is stored or processed outside the Philippines, we implement appropriate safeguards consistent with the DPA and NPC guidance.

4) Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to achieve the purposes above and to meet legal or regulatory requirements. We securely delete or anonymize data when no longer needed.

5) Security Measures

We adopt organizational, physical, and technical safeguards proportionate to the risks, including access controls, secure communications, encryption where appropriate, and regular review of security practices.

6) Your Data Subject Rights

You have rights under the DPA, including to:

  • Be informed about processing activities
  • Access and obtain a copy of your personal data
  • Object to, or request restriction of, processing in certain circumstances
  • Request correction, updating, or deletion of inaccurate/outdated data
  • Withdraw consent without affecting prior lawful processing
  • File a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC)

7) How to Exercise Your Rights / Contact

For requests or concerns, please email our contact below. We may require information to verify your identity and will respond within reasonable periods consistent with NPC rules.
